Hi, I’m Edward

Imagine you’re browsing a bookstore and a guy walks up, says “Hi, I’m Edward,” and then proceeds to deliver this entire document as a pickup line. This is basically that, just with paragraph breaks, pictures, and the option to close the tab.

The basics

In real life, most conversations start with my height. Here, I get to pretend that’s not my entire personality.

Who I am

I’m a 29-year-old research engineer in Boston. I initially come across as pretty laid-back, but I think it’s more that I like giving conversations room to go somewhere real. I love the moment when someone drops out of small talk and starts talking about what’s genuinely been on their mind lately. I also really like becoming a regular somewhere: getting to know baristas, recognizing people around the neighborhood, slowly feeling connected to a place over time.

Relationships

I’m still figuring out exactly what shape I want a long-term relationship to take. My last relationship naturally settled into a “one person in medical school, the other supporting” dynamic, and while I’m grateful for that chapter of my life, I think I want something more mutual and exploratory moving forward. Writing this document was partially a way for me to reconnect with the parts of myself that I want to bring into my next relationship.

The rest of the document

I found three themes that run through most parts of my life: building things with care, trying to meet people with kindness, and being willing to do the uncomfortable work of growing. I think they give a pretty good window into who I am, so I describe them in terms of archetypes in the following section.

My personality in archetypes

Overview

A friend once told me that there are two ways to succeed in life: be smart, or be persistent. Most of the things I’m proud of in my life came from persistence. I tend to build routines around the things that matter most to me—work, meditation, exercise, friendships, relationships—and trust that the small daily effort adds up over time.

Upsides

In romantic relationships, this shows up as me being steady and dependable. I remember things, follow through, pay attention to patterns, and keep putting energy into the relationship even after the initial excitement settles down.

Tradeoffs

I genuinely enjoy structure and consistency in my daily life. I like exercising regularly, meditating every day, eating reasonably well, and having routines that help me feel grounded. People who prefer a much more spontaneous or chaotic lifestyle might find me a little rigid.

Overview

I meditate for at least an hour every day, and Buddhism has shaped a lot of how I move through the world. I’m less interested in Buddhism as a set of metaphysical beliefs and more interested in it as a daily practice of attention, kindness, and not taking myself too seriously. One of the biggest things it’s given me is the ability to stay present with people and respond thoughtfully instead of reactively.

Upsides

I get along well with most people and genuinely enjoy talking to them. I’m equally comfortable with silly banter and deep emotional conversations, and I think people tend to feel pretty seen and accepted around me.

Tradeoffs

I care a lot about maintaining connection with people, and the downside is that I can sometimes hesitate to express my own needs if I think it might create conflict. This is something I’ve worked on a lot over the years, and I’m continuing to get better at it.

Overview

I care a lot about looking at myself honestly. For me, that’s looked like therapy, journaling, and building close friendships with men who genuinely encourage each other to grow. I’ve found that most meaningful growth tends to come from being willing to face uncomfortable emotions and have difficult conversations instead of avoiding them.

Upsides

I think this work has made me more self-aware, emotionally honest, and willing to engage directly with difficult things instead of avoiding them. I also put a lot of effort into maintaining close relationships with people who genuinely want the best for each other.

Tradeoffs

I tend to feel closest to people who are comfortable talking openly about emotions, patterns, and inner life. When that shared language isn’t there, I can sometimes feel a little disconnected. I’m also aware that parts of this world can sound a bit “woo-woo” from the outside.

Weird Events

I absolutely love strange and absurd events. If you tell me there’s a tantra workshop, an experimental art exhibit, or some obscure meditation gathering happening nearby, there’s a decent chance I’ll immediately rearrange my weekend around it.

⌬

Aggressively Personalized Technology

I have a tendency to optimize my technology for focus in ways that other people find deeply concerning. My phone is grayscale, doesn’t have a web browser installed, and is intentionally designed to be kind of boring. My computer is held together by a maze of keyboard shortcuts and custom workflows that make it extremely efficient for me and completely unusable for everyone else.

⚙

Factorio Corporate Culture

A group of friends and I once became so committed to a Factorio save file that we accidentally developed a small corporate culture around managing our fake factory. There were planning meetings. There were performance reviews. And, at the end of every shift, one person would receive the deeply dishonorable “Least Valuable Player” designation.
